## Overview
Legroom Design Optimization was a project I developed for my Probability and Statistics course. 
I demonstrated the application of normal distribution to solve an ergonomic challenge: determining the optimal legroom threshold based on male height distribution. 
The goal was to ensure high comfort for over 90% of passengers while balancing manufacturing efficiency and production constraints.

## Methodology
- defining the clarity of problems and goals.
- literature review.
- define the random variable.
- get the data distribution using random generator based on the theoritical parameter.
- prove that the distribution pass the normality test.
- get an insight of distribution with empirical rule.
- implement CDF and PDF to solve the problem.

## Result
The legroom design, based on a 190 cm threshold for adult males, yields an inclusivity rate of 93.83%, leaving only a 6.17% risk of discomfort for individuals with extreme height percentiles. 
Analytically, this threshold is considered optimal as it accommodates the vast majority of the population without compromising cabin capacity. 
As a supplementary recommendation, this 6% outlier group can be directed toward specialized seating with extra legroom, ensuring mass-production efficiency while maintaining universal comfort standards.
